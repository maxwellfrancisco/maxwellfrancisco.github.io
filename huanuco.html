Mapping the Inca site of Huánuco Pampa

Tools: ArcGIS Pro, Georeferencing, Digitization, Adobe Illustrator

Overview

This map was produced for a poster presentation at the UT 2025 Undergraduate Research Apprenticeship Symposium. It was created to help the audience visualize the site and its location within Peru. Further work is to be done analyzing how the spatial distribution of artifacts across the site show the influence of space in the production process of weaving.

The aqllawasi was an Inca institution of cloistered religious women across the Andes, serving to educate young girls in Inca crafts such as brewing and weaving, and project imperial power. The aqllawasi at Huánuco Pampa is the best surviving archaeological example of these institutions making it highly valuable for study.

Data

  • Map of the site in the form of non-spatially referenced image
  • Artifact images from the site archive

Methods

  • Georeferencing of the site from an image with no spatial reference.
  • Digitization of the aqllawasi at the site, including buildings and walls, and labeling with attribute table.
  • Adobe Illustrator in order to add artifact images and final aesthetic polishing for presentation.
