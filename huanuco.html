Mapping the Inca site of Huánuco Pampa

Tools: ArcGIS Pro, Adobe Illustrator, Georeferencing, Digitization

Overview

This map was produced for the 2025 Undergraduate Research Apprentinceship Program Symposium for a poster presentation. Further work will be done to analyze how the spatial distribution of artifacts across the site contributed and are evidence of the production process of weaving.

The aqllawasi was an Incan institution of cloistered religious women across the Andes, serving to educate young girls and project Inca imperial power. The aqllawasi at Huánuco Pampa is the best surviving archaeological example of these institutions.

Data

  • Map of the site in the form of non-spatially referenced image
  • Artifact images from the site archive

Methods

  • Georeferencing of the site from an image with no spatial reference.
  • Digitzation of the Aqllawasi at the site, including buildings and walls.
  • Adobe Illustrator in order to add artifact images and final polishing for presentation.
